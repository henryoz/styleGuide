Forms

Overview

One of the primary points of interaction with the Simitive product is via forms and their elements. Users are required to manage their profiles, fill out lengthy reviews, manage allocations and other tasks, all of which require manual input of information. Our goal is to provide forms that are intuitive both in their visual presentation and flow. We must also offer appropriate, informative guidance and feedback to guide users through the process with zero confusion.

Our forms must also be accessible via touch, keyboard, and mouse input and be parsable by tools such as screen readers and magnifiers.

Labels

  • Every form field must have a concise, descriptive label
  • Labels should be positioned above their relevant field
  • Labels should be associated with form fields using the for attribute

Mandatory & optional fields

  • Only require fields where absolutely necessary. Mandatory fields must be marked with a 'mandatory' label
  • Optional fields must be marked with an 'optional' label, except in cases where all fields on a form are optional

Layout

  • Fields should be ordered sensibly and in accordance with their relative importance
  • Fields should take up space proportional to their contents
  • All fields should take up 100% width on smaller screen sizes
  • Related fields should be grouped by a fieldset tag with an appropriate label

Focus

  • Hover behaviour is best left to its defaults
  • Any form elements that change on :hover should also change on :focus
  • Focused elements are marked by a 3px blue border, as shown in the examples below
