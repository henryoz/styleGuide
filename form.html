Simitive Style & Usage

Forms

Overview

One of the primary points of interaction with the Simitive product is via forms and their elements. Users are required to manage their profiles, fill out lengthy reviews, manage allocations and other tasks, all of which require manual input of information. Our goal is to provide forms that are intuitive both in their visual presentation and flow. We must also offer appropriate, informative guidance and feedback to guide users through the process with zero confusion.

Our forms must also be accessible via touch, keyboard, and mouse input and be parseable by tools such as screen readers and others as defined by the Open Accessibility Framework.

This section of the guide covers basic appearance and functionality as it relates to our forms and their elements, using explanations and examples.

Form Elements

Text

By far the most versatile and widely used form element, text fields allow users to enter free text for a large variety of purposes. Standard text fields take up a single line (multi-line text entry is handled by the separate textarea element) and may vary in horizontal size, occupying the most appropriate span of the view as is required.

Text Fields

Navigating through text fields may be done via touching, clicking or tabbing through elements. It must be clear to the user at all times where they are in the form.
